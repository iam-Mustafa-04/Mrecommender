Iron Man
Lots of superhero movies are, at their core, also science fiction movies, but the first Marvel Cinematic Universe film - which takes place at a time when the MCU’s fictional reality seemed relatively real, and the introduction of superheroes was a major revelation - is probably the most straightforward example. Iron Man isn’t just a movie about a guy in a cool costume fighting supervillains. It’s rooted in the fundamental irony of Stan Lee, Larry Lieber, Don Heck and Jack Kirby’s original character, an arms manufacturer who is forced to turn himself into a weapon and experience firsthand the moral complexities of warfare, and come to terms with his own culpability in making the world a dangerous place.

DIRECTOR: Jon Favreau
WRITER: Mark Fergus, Hawk Ostby, Art Marcum
STARS: Robert Downey Jr. , Gwyneth Paltrow , Terrence Howard
YEAR: 2008

Gamer
The horrifying dystopian vision of humans hunting humans for sport takes on a new dimension in Neveldine/Taylor’s energized and embittered sci-fi thriller Gamer. The film stars Gerard Butler as a convicted felon on death row who has a slim chance to earn his freedom by taking part in modern gladiatorial combat for the entertainment of the masses. The twist is, he’s not in control of his own body: he’s being controlled by a teenaged video game enthusiast, who gleefully uses our hero to slaughter other criminals for fun and profit.

Splice
It’s nothing short of astounding how many different, horrifying ways scientists have found to play god. In fiction, at least, they’ve been revisiting the shocking biological experiments of Mary Shelley’s Frankenstein for over two centuries, frequently concocting new and unthinkable creatures and rarely, if ever, considering the awesome responsibility of their actions. Vincenzo Natali’s impeccably crafted and obscenely icky Splice may very well be the creepiest modern rendition of the timeless classic.

Inception
Christopher Nolan's riff on Satoshi Kon's Paprika borrows ideas and imagery but becomes its own, distinct entity, full of new ideas and stunning imagery. Inception stars Leonardo DiCaprio as a thief who steals ideas from within his victim’s dreams, who has been hired to do the exact opposite: introduce a brand new idea into the brain of a corporate tycoon. That’s harder than it sounds, and so he assembles a team of crackerjack geniuses to build mazes within mazes inside their mark’s mind, and dig ever deeper into his subconscious to plant the seeds that must be planted.

TRON: Legacy
Audiences werent ready for TRON when the original film came out in 1982. The ultra-stylized, neon-highlighted sci-fi/fantasy starred Jeff Bridges as a programmer who got sucked into the inner world of a computer’s operating system, where anthropomorphic programs worship their unseen users like gods. As a film, the original was full of heady ideas and amazing imagery. It was also admittedly rather stodgy, but that’s not something you say about TRON: Legacy.

Edge of Tomorrow
Most action movies do every single thing in their power not to kill their protagonist, but that’s not the case in Doug Liman’s insidiously clever Edge of Tomorrow. The film stars Tom Cruise as a military publicist sent to the front lines and forced to fight for his life against an alien armada, and he dies almost immediately. The gag is he then goes back to the start of his day, reliving all the same events all over again, and dies almost immediately. In fact, Tom Cruise dies so much in Edge of Tomorrow that the film probably has one of the largest body counts in years… and the victim is always Tom Cruise.

DIRECTOR: Doug Liman
WRITER: Christopher McQuarrie,Jez Butterworth,John-Henry Butterworth
STARS: Tom Cruise,Emily Blunt,Bill Paxton

Guardians of the Galaxy
It took a surprisingly long time for the Marvel Cinematic Universe - a franchise which argues that all magic is science, and that even the mythological gods of yore are simply space aliens with familiar names - to finally tell a story that takes place completely in outer space. But when they did, they did it right. James Gunn’s Guardians of the Galaxy combines the wry wit that came to define the MCU with old-fashioned space opera spectacles, in which a ragtag team of misfits have to set aside their differences to save the universe from a space MacGuffin that could kill us all. Interstellar
Christopher Nolans ambitious sci-fi epic Interstellar may not have been the 2001: A Space Odyssey of its generation, but it may forever change the way we look at space travel in cinema. Matthew McConaughey stars as a former astronaut in a near future overtaken by famine, when life on Earth will soon be impossible, and only a last-ditch effort to find a new home amongst the stars can save the mankind. The crew’s mission is to search the few potentially inhabitable planets within reach in the hopes of finding a new home.
